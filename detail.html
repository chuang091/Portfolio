Average Nearest Neighbor

This research article explores the question, "Why do different brands of convenience stores tend to locate near each other?" We incorporate the concept of Hotelling's model of spatial competition and employ the Average Nearest Neighbor (ANN) method to substantiate our findings.

Moran's I and LISA

This article introduces Moran's Index, which we apply to compare population density and random variables. We calculate their respective Moran's Indices to assess spatial correlation. Furthermore, we employ Local Indicators of Spatial Association (LISA) to identify areas with relatively lower or higher values compared to their neighboring features.

Convenience store research

This research is intended for participation in this year's national GIS contest. It focuses on identifying patterns of high convenience store density in Taipei and investigating potential variables that may influence the decision to open a convenience store or not.

Spatial Regression

The map displayed below illustrates the residuals of Multi-scale Geographically Weighted Regression (MGWR) and Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) results for convenience store locations. It is evident that MGWR (left) is more discrete than OLS (right), and MGWR's R-squared value is higher than that of OLS. This indicates that MGWR offers greater explanatory power compared to OLS.

Suitability Analysis

The map shown below demonstrates the application of a suitability modeler in ArcGIS Pro. We utilize five variables for this analysis, encompassing metro, bus, and public bicycle system accessibility, population density, and road node density. Additionally, I have applied the results to a 3D representation to enhance clarity and visualization.

Space Time Cube

The map displayed below forecasts convenience store counts for the next three years per quadrat using Space Time Pattern Mining Tools. Each quadrat has its own time series, and we employ three types of forecasting methods: random forest, curve fitting, and exponential smoothing. Ultimately, we select the best-fitting method to represent the predictions for each quadrat, as shown on the Right.
The distribution of the chosen prediction methods is depicted on the left.
